Day 1 - Monday, January 19, 2026 (10AM-12PM)

Learning Objectives

By the end of the session, you are:

  • Excited about ML for Neural Interfaces (both the field and the class)
  • Aware of the scope, logistics, grading criteria and outline of this course
  • Able to articulate how the person's conscious experience and intent interacts with measurable physical processes in the brain, and vice versa
  • Aware of the current State of the Art in Brain-Computer Interfaces
  • Acquainted with the bare basics of the dynamical systems framework and of computational architecture of the brain: neurons, synapses, sensory and motor pathways
  • Equipped with knowledge of how intracranial electroencephalography (iEEG) works and how it relates to ongoing processes in the brain
  • Prepared for succeeding with the first lab assignment: loading and manipulating human neural data

Lecture (10:05 - 11:15 AM): Machine Learning for Neural Interfaces

  • Welcome speech
  • Axioms of BCI
  • Manifestation of conscious experience in the brain
  • Eddy Chang speech decoding demo
  • Neuralink cursor & robot demo
  • Modulation of conscious experience through brain stimulation
  • SEEG stimulation demo (amygdala stimulation)
  • Logistics and scope of the class (content, grading, etc)
  • Basics of neural computation: neurons, neural circuits, sensory and motor pathways
  • Types of signals (spikes, EEG, iEEG, sEEG, ECoG, EMG etc.)
  • Hardware
  • Why neural data are different from standard ML data (noise, artifacts)
  • Challenges in brain decoding (representation drift, cross-session, cross-task generalization)

Guest Speaker (11:15 - 11:55 AM): Dr. Joshua Aronson

  • Motivation talk
  • Overview of acquisition hardware (EEG caps, depth electrodes, grids, stentrodes)
  • Demo of iEEG devices
  • Real-world clinical use of iEEG
  • Patient outcomes
  • Q&A with students

Day 2 - Tuesday, January 20, 2026 (10AM-12PM)

Learning Objectives

By the end of the session, you are:

  • Equipped with a repertoire of signal processing and modeling methods for iEEG that you can select and evaluate based on the problem at hand
  • Comfortable to start using open source tools and benchmarks: neuroprobe, temporaldata, brainsets, torch-brain, crane
  • Know about the largest open source iEEG datasets and how to access them in crane through ieeg-data with brainsets
  • Prepared to succeed on the second lab assignment: training a small foundation model of neural activity

Lecture (10:05 - 11:15 AM): Decrypting the Neural Code with ML

  • Overview of the ML pipeline in BCI (data → preprocess → model → evaluation)
  • Signal pre-processing of iEEG data
  • ML paradigms for BCI and examples:
    • Supervised (motor intent decoding)
    • Unsupervised (clustering of seizure vs healthy events)
    • Self-supervised (next brain sequence prediction)
  • Open-source software stacks (neuroprobe, temporaldata, brainsets, torch-brain, crane, MNE)
